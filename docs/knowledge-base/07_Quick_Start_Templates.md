## 2. Meeting Templates

### Kickoff Meeting Agenda

**Meeting**: [Program Name] Kickoff  
**Date**: [Date]  
**Duration**: [Duration]  
**Location**: [Location/Virtual]  
**Facilitator**: [Name]

**Objectives**:
- Align stakeholders on program goals and approach
- Establish team structure and communication norms
- Create shared understanding of success

**Agenda**:

**1. Welcome and Introductions** (10 min)
- Introductions round-robin
- Meeting objectives

**2. Program Overview** (20 min)
- Business case and strategic alignment
- Program objectives and success criteria
- Scope and key deliverables
- Timeline and major milestones

**3. Organization and Governance** (15 min)
- Team structure and roles
- RACI matrix
- Decision-making process
- Governance structure

**4. Communication Plan** (10 min)
- Status reporting cadence
- Meeting schedule
- Communication channels
- Escalation process

**5. Risk and Issues** (10 min)
- Initial risk assessment
- Risk management approach
- Issue escalation process

**6. Q&A and Discussion** (15 min)
- Open questions
- Concerns or clarifications

**7. Next Steps and Closing** (10 min)
- Immediate next actions
- First milestone and deliverables
- Follow-up items

### Status Review Meeting Agenda

**Meeting**: [Program Name] Status Review  
**Date**: [Date]  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Attendees**: [List]

**Pre-Read**: Status report distributed 24 hours before meeting

**Agenda**:

**1. Progress Review** (15 min)
- Key accomplishments since last meeting
- Metrics and KPIs update
- Milestone progress

**2. Issues Deep-Dive** (20 min)
- Issue 1: [Description] - [Owner presentation]
- Issue 2: [Description] - [Owner presentation]
- Resolution approaches and decisions needed

**3. Risk Review** (10 min)
- Changes to risk profile
- New risks identified
- Mitigation progress

**4. Upcoming Activities** (10 min)
- Next period priorities
- Dependencies and coordination needs
- Resource requirements

**5. Decisions and Actions** (5 min)
- Document decisions made
- Assign action items with owners and dates

### Retrospective Meeting Template

**Meeting**: [Phase/Sprint] Retrospective  
**Date**: [Date]  
**Duration**: 90 minutes  
**Facilitator**: [Name]

**Objectives**:
- Reflect on what worked and what didn't
- Identify actionable improvements
- Celebrate successes

**Agenda**:

**1. Set the Stage** (5 min)
- Review objectives
- Establish psychological safety ground rules
- Remind: focus on processes, not people

**2. Gather Data** (20 min)
- Timeline of key events
- Metrics review (velocity, quality, satisfaction)
- Individual reflections

**3. Generate Insights** (30 min)
**Structured Discussion**:
- What went well? (Continue doing)
- What didn't go well? (Stop doing)
- What should we try? (Start doing)

**4. Decide What to Do** (25 min)
- Prioritize improvement ideas
- Select 2-3 actions to implement
- Assign owners and define success criteria
- Create action plan

**5. Close** (10 min)
- Recap action items
- Appreciation round
- Feedback on retrospective process itself

## 8. AI-Enhanced Workflow Templates

### Weekly AI-Assisted Review Workflow

**Every Monday Morning**:

1. **Compile Information** (15 min)
   - Gather status updates from team
   - Review task completion from last week
   - Check risk register
   - Review stakeholder feedback

2. **AI Analysis** (5 min)
   ```
   Analyze this program information for trends and concerns:
   [paste compiled information]
   
   Provide:
   - Key trends (positive and negative)
   - Emerging risks
   - Items requiring my attention
   - Suggested priorities for this week
   ```

3. **Executive Summary Draft** (5 min)
   ```
   Create an executive summary for [stakeholder] based on:
   [paste information and AI analysis]
   
   Keep to 3 paragraphs:
   - Overall status and key achievements
   - Concerns and risks
   - What's needed from stakeholder
   ```

4. **Plan Week** (10 min)
   - Review AI suggestions
   - Set priorities
   - Schedule key conversations
   - Assign focus areas to team

### Monthly Deep Analysis Workflow

**Last Week of Each Month**:

1. **Risk Deep-Dive** (30 min)
   ```
   Comprehensive risk analysis for [Program Name]:
   Current risk register: [paste]
   Recent developments: [paste month's major events]
   
   Identify:
   - Risks that should be upgraded/downgraded
   - Emerging risks not yet on register
   - Mitigation strategies that need strengthening
   - Risks that can be closed
   ```

2. **Stakeholder Relationship Review** (20 min)
   ```
   Review stakeholder engagement for [Program]:
   Stakeholder interactions this month: [paste summary]
   Feedback received: [paste]
   
   Assess:
   - Which relationships need attention
   - Communication that worked well
   - Communication that needs improvement
   - Concerns I should address proactively
   ```

3. **Bias Check** (15 min)
   ```
   Review our program assumptions for biases:
   Current plan: [paste key elements]
   This month's decisions: [paste]
   
   Check for:
   - Optimism bias in estimates
   - Confirmation bias in data interpretation
   - Sunk cost thinking in any decisions
   - Groupthink in team consensus
   ```

4. **Next Month Planning** (30 min)
   - Synthesize insights from AI analyses
   - Update program plans based on findings
   - Prepare communications for stakeholders
   - Set focus areas for next month

## 10. Customization Guide

### How to Adapt These Templates

1. **Add Your Branding**: Logo, colors, formatting
2. **Adjust Terminology**: Match your organization's language
3. **Modify Sections**: Add/remove based on your needs
4. **Set Standards**: Define what "good" looks like for each template
5. **Create Examples**: Fill in templates with example content
6. **Get Feedback**: Test with team and refine
7. **Version Control**: Track changes to templates over time

### Template Library Maintenance

**Quarterly Review**:
- Which templates are being used?
- Which are ignored?
- What new templates are needed?
- How can existing templates be improved?
- Are AI prompts still effective?

**Continuous Improvement**:
- Collect feedback after each use
- Update based on what works
- Share best practices across programs
- Archive outdated versions
- Document why changes were made
